July 8th, 2018

Quick update here -

After working offline tinkering about with the to-be-renamed Cynobroad chat program, I feel like it's at a point where it reaches a few requirements of mine to be published: A) It isn't entirely made up of code from StackOverflow, and B) it at least somewhat doesn't resemble a moist pile of non-working electronic waste. The GitHub repo is available here. Here's the rundown about what the program's gone through development-wise so far.

Originally, I intended it to use UDP - it's quick and easy to work with, and suits my needs as I had planned to distribute this software to friends to use on a local network, thereby not requiring a server. Heh.. yeah, unfortunately not. Although, now I can confirm all the jokes on UDP are true. I'd tell you some, but I don't think you'd get them.

This, of course, left TCP as one of my main options. From what I'd seen, TCP was a right pain to work with compared to UDP, although I did locate a few helpful sources that greatly simplified the information I needed to know. Since all I really needed was a small decentralized thing that could run anywhere, using a TCP client/server relationship seemed a little overkill, but I went with it anyway as it seemed useful and worthwhile enough to spend the time to learn how to implement it.

And that's where the program stands today. It's still a very very early stages sort of thing, with a basically non-functional UI apart from the chat system, and there's no way to distinguish who's who and who said what. Despite this, since the main difficult bit is out of the way (Networking is hard, okay?), more regular updates will be following.

That's it, see you next time.

July 4th, 2018

Happy fourth of July, fellow Americans! SDProductions has two announcements, one for a new peice of off-brand kit to add to your digitalized collection, and another for our plush-merchant roadmap! That's right, now you can feel even better as you eat your hot dogs and your hopefully-not-overcooked steaks. Nothing is worse than an overcooked steak.

So you're probably wondering - "What do you have for us now, glorious off-brand overlords SDProductions?" Well, glad you asked! Introducing Cynobroad, our alternative to the growing competing market that is the chat service. We saw that Discord had taken off massively within the cyberspace, Steam was doing a blatant ripoff of Discord in their new beta (Sorry Steam, but it was too obvious), and services like Slack also doing quite well. So, we decided to make our own crappy off-brand of these services!

Cynobroad is still within testing and basic design phases at the moment - which means code copied from StackOverflow and buggy and unusable things both in the code and out. We're just taking a look at what we'd like our program to do that the other services don't offer and I personally am learning the concepts behind this, like the networking bits and how that all fits together. For a chat service, that's pretty important. As a result, Cynobroad will not see a GitHub repository for a to be determined amount of time. It may also under go some rebranding, so keep an eye out!

Our second announcement is our roadmap for our software. We do have a plan for it, and we'd like to let you know what it is.

Currently, our main thing we've been working on is Trackr, our off-brand time-management solution, and now we are transitioning to mainly work on Cynobroad. After about 3-4 of these little apps get released, we'd like to start work upon an app overlay including these apps and some other features. While we don't know exactly what we'd like of the overlay, we do know it should fit the theme of our programs, have a sleek and unobstructive design, and be able to support future third-party expandability, be that programs or an in-built editor. Overall, as we progress to this things may get redesigns and rebrands to further enforce a clean integration with eachother.

That's it for today! Have fun out there with your BBQ and stuff.


June 30th, 2018

We (singular) here at SDProductions are pleased to announce that Trackr, our off-brand time management application and helper, has entered the beta stages of development! If you haven't seen it, you should take a look.

While other similar services require you to sign up and sync data to their web-app equivalent, Trackr's desktop client never connects to any first, second, third, or fourth party servers. Or any, in fact. This means that you can be rest assured that whatever you put in never gets seen by anyone that doesn't have access to your computer. Great, right?

But wait, you ask. Even if Trackr never connects to any server, it can still have the potential to screw with my computer locally, right? First of all, I feel very offended you even thought that my software would be malware. And second, I'll tell you exactly what Trackr accesses from your system!


Trackr can access the following:

Your system's time and date

File data within Trackr's folders


And that's it! It doesn't screw with anything else. Not convinced? The source code is all available on the project's GitHub page.
